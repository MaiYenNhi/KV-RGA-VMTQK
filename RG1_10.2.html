About District 3

District 3

District 3 is located in the central part of Ho Chi Minh city.

District 3 combines French colonial architecture with modern-day urban development. It's home to iconic landmarks like the War Remnants Museum, Jade Emperor Pagoda, and Tan Dinh Church, and some top schools like Nguyen Thi Minh Khai High School or Marie Curie High school, which attract both tourists and locals alike. So we call it a home of ancient buildings.

Moreover, many French colonial-era structures that are eye-grabbing and fascinating live here. You can see them in ancient villas on Tran Quoc Thao, Ba Huyen Thanh Quan, and Tu Xuong Street.

Besides, some buildings resemble the styles of Japan, and China. For instance, a miniature Japanese town in Le Thanh Tong street, Jade Emperor Pagoda in Mai Thi Luu street.

Tan Dinh Church

It was built during the Frenchcolonial period in the 1870s and completed in 1876, features a blend of neo-Romanesque, neo-Gothic, and neo-Renaissance architectural styles. Since 1957, it has been painted pastel pink inside and out, earning the nickname “the pink church.”

It is the second-largest church in Ho Chi Minh City after Notre-Dame Basilica, both of which are part of the Archdiocese of Ho Chi Minh City. Unlike Notre-Dame (Nhà thờ Đức Bà), Tân Định Church is not a cathedral.

Turtle Lake (Hồ con rùa)

Turtle Lake (Hồ Con Rùa) has been through many changes both in design and function throughout the history of Saigon.

First, it housed a gate for a Nguyễn-dynasty citadel, then the land was flattened for a water tower in accordance with a new urban plan by the French colonial government, and finally, the square became a diplomatic display for past allies of the previous regime.

Local Life

  1. Local Life and Daily Rhythms

    2. Unlike the tourist-heavy District 1, District 3 feels more like a place where people live their everyday lives. You will hear the sounds of street vendors calling out their wares, smell the aroma of grilled meat and fresh herbs from local kitchens, but also still see the dynamic range of shops, centers and traditional buildings.

    What makes District 3 truly special is its community atmosphere. Despite being so close to the city center, it retains a distinctly local vibe.

    In the mornings, you will see people on motorbikes heading to work, vendors setting up their food stalls, and children in uniforms heading to school.

    The afternoons are often quieter, with locals taking a break from the midday heat but still the district is vibrant through trucks and big vehicles still working under the afternoon sun.

    In the evening, the district comes alive again, as families gather for dinner at open-air eateries, and young people meet up at cafes or karaoke bars.

  2. The culinary brilliance of Saigon

    3. District 3 is a food lover’s haven, where both traditional cuisine and trending meals take place. Local eateries are scattered in every corner, coming in a variety of meal types from sweet doughs to salty meat. For instance, a local favorite is banh mi, the iconic Vietnamese sandwich.

    You can visit Banh Mi Hoa Ma, one of the oldest banh mi shops in the city, known for its crispy baguettes stuffed with various meats, pate, and fresh vegetables for a wonderful traditional taste of the local daily life and Vietnamese history.

    Another must-try dish type in the area is phở, bún and bánh canh, the classic Vietnamese noodle soups. While you can find these noodles almost anywhere in Saigon, several well-known names amongst the locals and tourists are Hủ tiếu Nam Giang, Phở quậy Kiến Xây, Hủ tiếu Nhân quán,... with traditional broth recipes to bring out the most wonderful of flavors

  3. A creativity-fostering community

    4. In recent years, District 3 has become a hub for Saigon’s creative scene. Art galleries, cultural spaces, and indie shops are popping up throughout the district, reflecting the city’s dynamic culture. Not only are art spaces growing rapidly, local brands are becoming more present and loved, creating a dynamic vibe in district 3. It is not only a great place to get a feel for the artistic pulse but also to spend hours shopping .

    The district is also home to some unique, small bookshops and boutiques, where you can find handmade goods, local crafts, and vintage clothes.

    The Vintage Emporium is a cozy spot where you can browse through old vinyl records, vintage homeware, and one-of-a-kind fashion pieces, all while enjoying a cup of coffee.

    Along with many old instrumental shops and eateries, it really mixes up tradition and modernity.

Food

Street food

These dishes are commonly enjoyed by students during afternoon snack breaks or in the mornings to fuel their energy before heading to classes. Many students appreciate the convenience and taste of these snacks, making them a popular choice for a quick bite. Additionally, manual laborers, workers often rely on these foods to provide the energy to get through a demanding workday.

Typically, these snacks are sold by street vendors scattered along the roads, especially near schools or areas with a high concentration of workers. One of the popular streets is Hoàng Sa - Trường Sa. This road stretches along the riverbank and features parks on the sidewalks, providing a convenient selling space for street vendors.

The prices of these snacks are quite affordable, suitable for the budgets of students and workers, ranging from approximately 15,000 VND to 30,000 VND.

Traditional food

Dishes commonly enjoyed by families and tourists and usually served for lunch and dinner.

Each dish is a distinctive, traditional part of Vietnamese family meals. They are often sold at lunch eateries or restaurants catering to tourists, as well as in food streets where dishes like cơm tấm, phở and more can be found. Meals typically contain a salty meal and a complimentary soup. They are not only concentrated in busy areas like trunk food (street food) but can be found throughout District 3, as they are not only popular but also casual.

Since these meals are for lunch and dinner, they cost more when compared to snacks, ranging from about 25,000 VND to 70,000 VND.

These meals not only retain the traditions of the Vietnamese’s casual meals but also a rich and dynamic cuisine.

  • Some popular types of traditional food

Noodles

Meals of different types of noodles eaten with a corresponding broth of it is own and various meat and vegetables is very famous and loved. The most well-known noodle dish must be phở and bún, loved for their delicious aste and dynamic ingredients.

Broken rice

Broken rice served with grilled pork, shredded pork skin, fried egg, besides that is a unique fish sauce (includes sugar, garlic, and chili) is a famous dish and widely loved by vietnamese and foreigners.

Family-cooked dishes

Those are meals typically found in casual families and family eateries. Soups such as canh chua, canh cải,… or braised food (commonly fish or any red meat), famously known diíhes may be braised fish and pork.

Drinking snacks/dishes

Drinking snacks are a necessary thing in Vietnamese culinary culture, often enjoyed during gatherings with friends and family. These are generally paired with beer or alcohol. It is a vital part of celebrations and parties, providing a feeling of relaxation and stress relief after an exhausting day of work.

To attract tourists, bars and snack restaurants are usually located in places with frequent visitors such as entertainment areas, markets, and commercial areas. They can also be found in narrow streets, creating a comfortable and welcoming atmosphere.

Because of the variety of snacks available, pricing can range from low to high, ranging from 20,000 VND to 200,000 VND each dish.

Entertainment

District 3, Ho Chi Minh City, is known for its lively mix of historical sites, trendy bars, and relaxation spots. Whether you are in the mood for an educational museum visit, a night out at a vibrant bar, or a relaxing spa session, District 3 has something to offer for everyone. Here are four great entertainment places you can explore:

  1. War Remnants Museum

    2. Address: 28 Võ Văn Tần, Ward 6, District 3, Hồ Chí Minh City

    The War Remnants Museum is one of Ho Chi Minh City’s most famous historical landmarks. It features a wide range of exhibits focusing on the Vietnam War, including photographs, military artifacts, and personal accounts from survivors. The museum offers a compelling look at the lasting impact of the war on Vietnam.

    Review: A visit to the War Remnants Museum is both enlightening and emotional. The detailed displays and powerful imagery provide deep insights into the war’s toll on the Vietnamese people. While the exhibits can be intense, the museum is a valuable destination for those wanting to understand Vietnam’s history. The content may not be suitable for young children, but it’s highly recommended for history enthusiasts and anyone interested in world conflicts.

  2. BiaCraft Artisan Ales

    3. Address: 1 Lê Ngô Cát St., Ward 7, District 3, Ho Chi Minh City

    BiaCraft Artisan Ales is a popular spot for beer lovers in District 3. Known for its wide selection of craft beers, this bar serves both local and international brews. With a cozy, relaxed atmosphere, it’s a perfect spot to hang out with friends, enjoy good drinks, and try some of the bar’s tasty food options.

    Review: BiaCraft is a great choice for a night out with friends or a casual evening. The craft beer selection is diverse and ever-changing, giving patrons plenty of options to explore. The ambiance is laid-back, and the staff is knowledgeable about their offerings, making it easy to find something you’ll enjoy. The food, including snacks like sliders and fries, complements the beer perfectly. It’s a must-visit spot in District 3 for anyone who loves a good brew and lively conversation.

  3. Golden Lotus Spa & Massage Club

    4. Address: 15 Thảo Điền St., Ward 6, District 3, Ho Chi Minh City

    Golden Lotus Spa & Massage Club is a top-rated spa offering a wide range of relaxation services. From traditional massages to full-body treatments, the spa provides a serene environment where you can unwind. Specializing in Korean-style massage therapies, Golden Lotus focuses on balancing body and mind, perfect for those looking to escape the city’s hustle and bustle.

    Review: Golden Lotus Spa is a tranquil retreat in the hear of District 3. The soothing atmosphere, coupled with skilled therapists, ensures a relaxing and rejuvenating experience. Whether you choose a hot stone massage, foot reflexology, or a deep tissue massage, the quality of service is excellent. The spa offers a luxurious yet affordable way to de-stress, making it an ideal stop after a day of sightseeing or work. The professional staff and peaceful ambiance make it a favorite among both locals and tourists.

  4. Noir. Dining in the Dark

    5. Address: 178 Hai Bà Trưng St., Phường 6, Quận 3, Ho Chi Minh City

    Noir. Dining in the Dark is an unforgettable dining experience where guests eat in complete darkness, guided by visually impaired staff. The concept is designed to heighten your other senses, allowing you to truly focus on the taste, smell, and texture of the food. This unique restaurant offers various set menus, and the dishes are a mystery until you finish dining.

    Review: Noir. Dining in the Dark is more than just a meal—it’s an immersive sensory experience. The food is expertly crafted, and not being able to see what you are eating adds an element of surprise and excitement. The staff are incredibly helpful, and their guidance throughout the meal makes the experience seamless. It’s a perfect choice for those looking for something adventurous and different, combining excellent cuisine with a meaningful experience. This dining concept also raises awareness about the challenges faced by visually impaired individuals, adding depth to the overall experience.
